The Problem With AI Today

75% of AI proof-of-concepts never reach production.

Not because the models failed.

Because the organisation wasn't designed to receive them.

This is the problem I have spent the last decade solving — at board level, at delivery level, and everywhere in between.

The Work

Three acts. One through-line.

I

Act I

AI Commercial Transformation

Reshaping how enterprises go to market with AI — from strategy to commercial model to delivery structure. Not a roadmap exercise. A full-stack rethink of how value is created and captured.

The AI story is easy to sell. The hard part is building an organisation that can keep the promise.

II

Act II

GTM & Revenue Architecture

Designing the commercial engine that turns AI capability into repeatable revenue. Pricing models, partner ecosystems, sales motion, and the metrics that actually predict growth.

Most AI GTM strategies are technology-out. The ones that scale are value-in.

III

Act III

AI Operating Models

Building the internal architecture that makes AI sustainable — governance, talent, data infrastructure, and the organisational design choices that determine whether transformation sticks.

An AI operating model is not an IT decision. It is a leadership decision that happens to involve technology.
